# PeriSmart Homepage Explainer Video Brief

**Level-2 Content Specification (L2)**  
**Version 1.0** | March 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the placeholder, script, storyboard, and production approach for a future homepage explainer video for the PeriSmart public website.

The initial website should include a **placeholder for this video**, not the final video asset.

---

## 2. Role of the Video

The explainer video should help a first-time visitor understand:
- the day-of-operations problem PeriSmart addresses
- how PeriSmart creates operational visibility
- how prediction and notification improve coordination
- why the privacy and deployment model is credible

The video is not intended to be:
- a product demo with detailed UI walkthrough
- a clinical training video
- a technical architecture deep dive

---

## 3. Placeholder Requirements

Until the final video exists, the homepage should reserve a visible 16:9 area with:
- poster-style image or graphic
- label such as `Product Overview Video`
- short text such as `A short explainer video will be added here.`
- optional caption listing the topics:
  - visibility
  - prediction
  - notifications
  - privacy-first deployment

The placeholder should look intentional, not like a broken embed.

---

## 4. Recommended Video Length

Recommended finished length:
- **60 to 90 seconds**

Target sweet spot:
- **about 75 seconds**

This is long enough to explain the product clearly and short enough to sit near the top of a public homepage.

---

## 5. Recommended Narrative Structure

### Scene 1: The operating-day problem
Focus:
- OR teams often rely on status chasing, walking, calls, and fragmented updates.

### Scene 2: Real-time visibility
Focus:
- PeriSmart shows what is happening now across rooms.

### Scene 3: Planned vs actual vs predicted
Focus:
- teams can see not only the schedule, but how the day is actually unfolding.

### Scene 4: Earlier coordination
Focus:
- notifications and runway help downstream teams prepare sooner.

### Scene 5: Privacy and deployment
Focus:
- de-identification happens at the edge and PeriSmart works with existing scheduling systems.

### Scene 6: Outcome and CTA
Focus:
- better operational coordination with a clear invitation to request a demo.

---

## 6. Recommended Script

### 6.1 Voiceover Draft

`Operating room teams should not have to manage the day by walking the halls, making calls, and guessing what happens next.`

`PeriSmart gives perioperative teams a real-time view of OR execution, with clear visibility into what is happening now, what has changed, and what is likely to happen next.`

`By combining schedule context with camera-based workflow awareness, PeriSmart helps teams see planned, actual, and predicted timelines in one operational picture.`

`That means earlier awareness of delays, better turnover coordination, and fewer manual status checks across pre-op, the OR, anesthesia, and PACU.`

`PeriSmart is designed for practical deployment, with edge-based de-identification and integration with existing scheduling systems rather than a full rip-and-replace approach.`

`The result is a more coordinated surgical day, with better visibility for the people responsible for flow.`

`Request a demo to see how PeriSmart can support your perioperative operations.`

### 6.2 On-Screen Text Suggestions

- Real-time OR execution visibility
- Planned. Actual. Predicted.
- Earlier coordination across teams
- Privacy-first deployment
- Request a Demo

## 8. Visual Style Guidance

Preferred style:
- modern and clean
- operational rather than cinematic-drama heavy
- de-identified and privacy-conscious
- slightly abstracted rather than photoreal close-up surgery footage

Avoid:
- graphic surgical imagery
- identifiable patients or clinicians
- overly futuristic AI tropes
- exaggerated hologram interfaces

---

## 9. GenAI Production Recommendation

### 9.1 Recommended primary workflow

Recommended approach:
1. create a short storyboard and visual style sheet first
2. generate individual scenes as short clips
3. edit the best clips together in a conventional editor
4. add voiceover, captions, music, and brand graphics in post

Do not try to create the entire finished explainer in one prompt.

### 9.2 Using Sora

Sora is the strongest fit for:
- short atmospheric scenes
- polished motion for homepage-quality hero visuals
- abstracted hospital operations sequences
- clean transitions between operational concepts

Recommended Sora workflow:
1. define one visual style for the full video
2. generate 6 to 8 clips of 5 to 8 seconds each
3. use locked prompt ingredients across clips:
   - same color palette
   - same environment style
   - same UI motif
   - privacy-safe hospital visuals
4. choose the best 1 or 2 variants per scene
5. finish the sequence in an editor such as Premiere Pro, Final Cut, CapCut, or Descript

### 9.3 Using "Nano Banana"-style generators

If you use a newer ad-video generator or rapid scene-generation tool in the "Nano Banana" category, use it for:
- fast concept boards
- alternate scene variations
- social cutdowns or short derivative clips

Do not rely on it as the only production tool unless it can maintain strong visual consistency across all scenes.

This recommendation is an inference from how these tools typically perform: they are often useful for rapid iteration, but consistency and polish still benefit from a storyboard-first workflow and final editing pass.

---

## 10. Recommended Prompt Ingredients

Use prompt ingredients such as:
- hospital operations command center
- de-identified OR workflow visuals
- clean healthcare operations dashboard
- minimal, trustworthy, modern interface overlays
- soft motion graphics for schedule, prediction, and notification cues
- no patient-identifying details
- no visible text that matters
- no dramatic surgery close-ups

Prompting should favor:
- medium-wide shots
- clean transitions
- readable composition
- brand-safe lighting and color

---

## 11. Practical Production Workflow

### Step 1: Lock message
- finalize the 60 to 90 second script

### Step 2: Build visual board
- define the look of rooms, dashboards, staff, and motion language

### Step 3: Generate clips
- create multiple short scenes with Sora or another generator

### Step 4: Edit conventionally
- assemble clips in timeline order
- add voiceover
- add captions and music
- insert static product mockups where needed

### Step 5: Export versions
- homepage version
- short social teaser
- silent captioned loop if desired

---

## 12. Acceptance Criteria

The explainer video concept is successful if:
- a new visitor can understand the product story in under 90 seconds
- the video remains within actual PeriSmart product scope
- the visuals feel professional without requiring live customer footage
- the video can be represented cleanly on a simple static HTML homepage

---

## 13. Recommendation Summary

For v1 of the public site:
- include a polished placeholder now
- plan for a 75-second explainer
- use Sora primarily for scene generation
- use a standard editor for assembly and finishing

This will produce a better result than waiting for a full live-action shoot or trying to generate the final video in a single pass.

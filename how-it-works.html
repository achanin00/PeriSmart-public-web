How It Works

Five steps from schedule context to earlier coordination.

PeriSmart fits into the surgical day as a straightforward operational layer: bring in planning context, understand actual room progression, update timing, and help teams coordinate sooner.

1

Bring in the day’s schedule

Use FHIR, HL7v2, CSV, or manual entry where needed. Schedule data provides planning context, not execution truth.

2

Capture actual room progression

PeriSmart uses camera-based workflow awareness to identify the milestones and room-state changes that matter for operations.

3

Update the OR picture in real time

The board can show planned, actual, and predicted timing together so staff can act on a clearer operational picture.

4

Notify the right people sooner

Alerts and OR activity subscriptions can support earlier preparation by room, area, service line, or surgeon.

5

Learn from what actually happened

Historical execution data supports analysis of turnover, block time utilization, room utilization, delays, idle time, and schedule accuracy.

Why This Framing Works

It keeps the story practical and credible.

  • PeriSmart works with existing scheduling systems instead of asking hospitals to start over.
  • The board and its timing model stay at the center of the product story.
  • Privacy can be explained clearly without turning the page into technical documentation.
